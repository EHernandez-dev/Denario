# Exercise Duration Estimates for Hands-On Modules

A careful review of the proposed course modules reveals that each module incorporates interactive, hands-on elements designed to reinforce learning through practical application, simulation, or role-play. The following estimates are based on the complexity of the activity, the expected level of participant interaction, and the pedagogical need for both active engagement and reflective debriefing.

---

## Module 1: Introduction to ChatGPT in Business Contexts

**Exercise:** Icebreaker and group discussion  
**Duration:** 15 minutes (activity), 5 minutes (debrief)  
**Justification:**  
A brief, low-complexity activity to surface participant experiences and set the stage for collaborative learning. The debrief allows for synthesis of shared insights and alignment of expectations.

---

## Module 2: Navigating ChatGPT Advanced Tools

**Exercise:** Guided walkthrough and paired tool exploration  
**Duration:** 25 minutes (activity), 10 minutes (debrief)  
**Justification:**  
Participants require sufficient time to explore the interface, experiment with uploading data, and use web search features. The paired format encourages peer learning. The debrief consolidates tool proficiency and addresses common challenges.

---

## Module 3: Prompt Engineering for Business Outcomes

**Exercise:** Prompt design workshop with peer feedback  
**Duration:** 30 minutes (activity), 10 minutes (debrief)  
**Justification:**  
Prompt engineering benefits from iterative practice and peer review. The allocated time supports multiple prompt iterations and collaborative critique, with a debrief to highlight best practices and lessons learned.

---

## Module 4: Data-Driven Decision Making with ChatGPT

**Exercise:** Simulation—data analysis and presentation  
**Duration:** 35 minutes (activity), 10 minutes (debrief)  
**Justification:**  
Analyzing data, generating visualizations, and preparing a brief presentation require extended hands-on time. The simulation format mimics real business tasks, and the debrief ensures that analytical insights are connected to decision-making.

---

## Module 5: Responsible AI Use and Managing Hallucinations

**Exercise:** Role-play scenario—managing AI misinformation and privacy  
**Duration:** 30 minutes (activity), 10 minutes (debrief)  
**Justification:**  
Role-play demands time for scenario immersion, group discussion, and ethical decision-making. The debrief is critical for unpacking ethical frameworks and reinforcing responsible AI practices.

---

## Module 6: Integrating ChatGPT into Business Workflows

**Exercise:** Group simulation—AI integration planning and presentation  
**Duration:** 40 minutes (activity), 15 minutes (debrief)  
**Justification:**  
Developing and presenting an AI integration plan is a complex, collaborative task. The extended duration allows for team ideation, workflow mapping, and presentation. The debrief supports cross-team learning and addresses implementation challenges.

---

## Module 7: Capstone Simulation and Action Planning

**Exercise:** Capstone simulation and action planning, peer review  
**Duration:** 45 minutes (activity), 15 minutes (peer review and debrief)  
**Justification:**  
The capstone requires synthesis of all course competencies in a realistic scenario, followed by individualized action planning. Peer review and debriefing are essential for feedback, reflection, and commitment to post-course application.

---
